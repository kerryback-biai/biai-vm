# Demo: AI as a Discussion Partner for Tradeoffs

## What This Demonstrates
- AI as a thinking partner for judgment calls (not just computation)
- Changing priorities changes the answer — the weights are YOUR choice
- AI can argue both sides and identify where reasonable people disagree
- The "Assess → Explore → Optimize → Discuss → Decide" framework

## Tool
Claude Code on AI+Code Lab (same session as previous demos)

## Prompts (conversational — try 2-3 of these)

### Prompt 1: Explain a Specific Tradeoff
```
The optimizer says to hold the AAPL lots even though AAPL is rated Sell. Walk me through the reasoning. What am I giving up by holding? What would it cost to sell?
```

### Prompt 2: Change the Budget
```
I'm willing to pay up to $5,000 more in taxes than the minimum if it significantly improves sector alignment. Re-run with that additional tax budget. How much better do the sector weights get?
```

### Prompt 3: New Information Changes Everything
```
The client just told me they expect to need $100K for college tuition in 5 years. How does that change which lots I should sell now vs. later? Should I be harvesting gains now while they're in a high bracket, or deferring to reduce current taxes?
```

### Prompt 4: Two Perspectives
```
Show me two plans side by side:
- Plan A: "Conservative" — minimize taxes above all else, tolerate sector drift
- Plan B: "Disciplined" — get sectors on target even if it costs more in taxes

For each plan: list the trades, total tax cost, and resulting max sector deviation. Which would you recommend for this client and why?
```

### Prompt 5: Final Recommendation
```
Given everything we've discussed — the tax costs, the sector drift, the college timeline, and the Sell-rated positions — what would you recommend? Explain your reasoning. Then tell me: where would a different wealth manager reasonably disagree with you?
```

## Key Narration Points
- "The optimizer found the MATH answer. But choosing the priorities is a JUDGMENT call."
- "Prompt 4 is powerful: seeing both plans side by side clarifies your own preferences."
- "Prompt 5 is the most important: 'where would someone reasonably disagree?' — that's the real decision point."
- "AI doesn't replace the decision. It accelerates the exploration and enriches the conversation."

## The Framework (write on whiteboard)
```
Assess → Explore → Optimize → Discuss → Decide
```
This applies to ANY multi-objective business decision, not just portfolios.

## Timing
~4 minutes (pick prompts 4 and 5 for live demo)
